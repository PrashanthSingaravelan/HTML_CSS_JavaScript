Apple Silicon

The Apple M1 is the first ARM-based System on a Chip (SoC) designed by Apple Inc. for its line of Macintosh computers.It appears in the fourth-generation MacBook Air, fifth-generation Mac Mini, and the 13-inch fifth-generation MacBook Pro.It is the first personal computer chip built using a 5 nm process. Apple states that it has the world's fastest central processing unit (CPU) core "in low power silicon" and the world's best CPU performance per watt.

CPU and GPU cores

The M1 has four high-performance and four low-power (dubbed "e-cores" or "high-efficiency") cores, providing a configuration similar to ARM big.LITTLE.
